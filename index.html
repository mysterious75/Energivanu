The Data Center Crisis

AI's insatiable appetite for power is creating a crisis that grid operators can no longer ignore. Global data center power demand is projected to reach 1,260 GW by 2030, with AI workloads consuming 820 GW.

Traditional demand response systems, designed for HVAC loads, cannot handle millisecond-scale volatility from massive GPU clusters running All-Reduce synchronization.

02

Neural Prediction

Energivanu uses a 613,000-parameter Temporal Convolutional Network with Multi-Head Self-Attention. It ingests 15 telemetry features across 30 timesteps and forecasts power consumption 10 steps ahead.

By predicting spikes before they happen, data centers can achieve ERCOT PCLR compliance and secure faster grid interconnection.

03

Phase Staggering

Instead of treating the data center as a black box, Energivanu reads training telemetry directly. It calculates optimal offsets for All-Reduce communication phases across GPU clusters.

By preventing clusters from synchronizing their highest-power operations, it drastically reduces aggregate grid volatility without slowing down the AI training process.
